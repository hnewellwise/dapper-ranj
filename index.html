Case: Part 2

The paid strategy

Demand generation: channels & approach

Fishing with a spear

Search advertising (Google & Microsoft Ads), intent-based, captures demand that already exists.

Fishing with a net

LinkedIn Ads, Meta, YouTube, content distribution, lead magnets, video. Targeting based on ICP profiles.

Budget

Working backwards from $2m revenue target (yr 2). 4 clients × $500k. 1% lead-to-client rate = 400 leads. At ~$1,000 CPL = ~$400k over 2 years.

Measurement

Sales → Opportunities → MQLs → Leads. Capture attribution at every stage; accept some leakage. Enterprise sales cycles may require 1–2 years of consistent activity.

Content

US-specific push using enterprise names US buyers recognise. Blogs, lead magnets, video demos. Lean into gamification: create content exciting enough to distribute organically.

Quick wins

Google Search Ads capture existing demand immediately while longer-term channels build momentum.

Content: Big idea

Gamification
& virality

What aspects of the product can we use to create content exciting enough to distribute organically? We want something that captures attention and builds the brand in the US without relying entirely on paid.

The principle

Build a short demo experience that shows the product working, make it emotionally engaging, shareable, and tied to a story people already care about.

The example: Artemis moon mission

A demo training programme showing what astronauts need to know for the Artemis flight control sequence. Framed as: "Anyone can go to the moon. They just need the right training."

Case: Part 3

The plot twist

⚠ Data alert

95% of US sessions are bots. The client had no idea, and based their US market entry decision partly on this data. Despite it, they're eager to move forward.

Validation: before scaling
  • Existing US clients, or EU clients with US entities?
  • Who are the competitors, and are they performing well?
  • CRM data: any organic US leads coming in already?
  • Run a small-scale test: ~$500 on Google & LinkedIn to validate real traffic and interest.
Strategy adjustment
  • Lower real traffic volume = lower existing demand than assumed.
  • Flip the emphasis: demand creation over demand capture.
  • Content distribution and awareness channels become more important, but take longer to pay off.
Case: Part 4

Managing the client relationship

The results aren't there yet. The client is frustrated. You're about to jump on a call, you need to pivot the strategy and hold the relationship together.

What to say to the client
  • Re-emphasise the playbook: demand creation takes time before demand capture kicks in.
  • Be sympathetic: understand the context. Is there internal pressure or company politics at play?
  • Offer closer involvement: decision making, creative review, results visibility, to rebuild confidence.
First strategy adjustment
  • Get more aggressive on Google Search bidding: raise CPA targets to push leads into the funnel faster.
  • Gives the sales team something to work with immediately and generates optimism on both sides while longer-term work matures.
