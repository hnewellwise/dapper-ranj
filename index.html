About the client

Who is &ranj?

&ranj is one of the biggest players in serious games in the Netherlands — and they outperform most competitors in the market.

Serious games drive behavioural change inside organisations. Think: new legislation passed, a long-standing process now has to work differently. Instead of handing employees a policy document, &ranj replicates real job situations in a game. Employees make decisions, bear the virtual consequences, and actually retain what they've learned.

Use case — Compliance

Helping organisations adopt new legislation or process changes through gameplay — not policy PDFs.

Use case — HR Onboarding

New employees face simulated, everyday scenarios relevant to their role. They make choices and see the outcomes — before day one.

Case — Part 1

The opportunity

&ranj has worked with the Dutch National Police, ING, Philips, and Bosch. Their own research points to a clear next step: the US market.

Traffic from the US is growing. The decision is made — the US is the country to conquer next. They've asked us to come in and assess what it would take.

The company asked us to see what the possibilities are. What would you — and &ranj — need in order to start working on a strategy to enter the US market?

Case — Part 2

Build the paid strategy

Research is done. &ranj has given the green light. Outline a paid marketing strategy to support the US launch.

  • Demand generation — which channels would you prioritise and why? How would you approach targeting?
  • Budget — what initial range would you propose, and how would you justify it to the client?
  • Measurement — how do you define and track success?
  • Content — what creative and content would you produce to support the strategy?
  • Quick wins — what can you implement immediately to capture existing demand?
Case — Part 3

The plot twist

The client grants you access to their Analytics account. The US traffic data that helped justify this whole decision? Take a closer look.

⚠ Data alert

95% of US sessions are bots. The client had no idea — and based their decision to enter the US market partly on this inflated data.

Despite the bad news, &ranj is eager to move forward.

How do you validate whether there is real US interest before scaling? And how do you adjust your performance strategy now that the data is known to be flawed?
