## Case - Part 1

&ranj has been working with several big clients in the Netherlands, from the Dutch National Police to ING, Philips, and Bosch. They’ve done some research themselves, they have decided the US is the country to conquer next.

**The company asked us to see what the possibilities are.**

What we can offer:
* account based marketing (ABM)
* demand generation
* demand capture

Also recommended:
* Events / conferences / sponsorship

**What would you/&ranj need in order to start working on a strategy for them to enter the US market?**

Need to see the following

* what are the goals? what does success look like? what is the purpose of the activity
* who are we targeting? what is the ICP? Target accounts
* What is the budget?
* when do you want to get started
* who are we working with? marketing, sales, website, content, social, growth?
* Who are the competitors?

## Case - Part 2

Outline a paid marketing strategy to support the US launch.

**Demand generation**

1. PPC - Google & Microsoft Ads
2. LinkedIn Ads - ABM & content distribution
3. Meta
4. Youtube

Fishing with a spear

* Search Advertising - based on intent

Fishing with a net

* Content distribution
* lead magnets
* video

Targeting based on ICP profiles.

**Budget**

Working backwards from sales goals.

Revenue target $2m by year 2.
4 x Clients worth $500k.
Lead to client conversion rate is 1%, so we need 400 leads in the next 2 years.
Cost of 1000 per lead is typical.
$400k budget

**Measurement**

Top level goal is sales then Opportunities, MQL, Leads.

Ideally all leads are attributed correctly, however we accept leaks. Key thing is to capture as much data at every stage.

Challenge: We are talking about high value contracts with enterprise companies that move slowly through sales cycle. We may require 1-2 years of consistent marketing activity to generate sales with target accounts. 

**Content**

Need a big push on US specific content, using enterprise corporations that US consumers know.
Blogs, lead magnets, video demos. etc

**Quick wins**

Google Search ads can capture existing demand quickly.

## Content Idea

Gamification / Virality
What aspects of the product can we use to create engaging content. We want content that is exciting enough to capture people's attention and get distributed organically.
Example:
Artemis moon mission captured huge global audience. We could make a short demo training programme to show how much information astronauts have to remember for the flight control sequence. Framed as "Anyone can go to the moon, they just need the right training."

## Case - Part 3

It turns out that 95% of the sessions are from “users” from the US who turn out to be bots. Despite that, they are eager to move forward.

**Validation**

* Clients - Does &RANJ have existing US clients, do their current EU clients have US entities that they can speak to?
* Competitors - Do we know who the main competitors are? Are they good?
* CRM Data - Where are leads coming from right now? If we are getting US leads organically this is a good sign.

Solution

* Run a small scale test (budget, geography, scope). Pure lead gen to get some sales calls and validate interest. $500 on Google Ads and LinkedIn to generate some quick clicks and we can validate the traffic.

**Adjustment**

* If the current traffic volume is much lower than expected we can assume that the demand from the US market may not be as high. So the strategy needs to flip the emphasis from demand capture to demand creation. 
* This means we might have to wait longer for the content distribution aspects to come into play, as there is less existing demand.

## Case - Part 4

The results the client was expecting are not there yet. Pivot the strategy, but you also need to manage the relationship.

**What would you say to the client at this moment?**

If we have only got to 3 weeks in and the client is facing issues then there have already been multiple failures on both sides to properly explain the context of this work and the logic behind the process. 
These kind of postmortem issues should have been laid out prior to the work starting.

* Re-emphasise the playbook - demand creation & demand capture
* Be sympathetic to concerns and try to understand the context of the problem. Is there internal pressure / company politics. 
* What is the solution? What does the client need that we can offer aside from results. Can we make them feel closer to the process with decision making or creative review or results visibility? 

**What would be the first thing you adjust in your paid strategy and why?**

* Be more aggressive on Google Search bidding (higher CPA targets) to get some leads in the funnel which will give the sales team something to work on and generate optimism within the team
